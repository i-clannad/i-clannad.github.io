A Little Little Story

It's an ordinary and true story, and maybe you're experiencing the same thing. This story is dedicated to all those who love or are loved.

It was about three years ago, when summer was over. However, the sun was still hot and the cicadas could still be heard from the trees. Hardly a pedestrian could be seen on the road.

I hurried to school -- though I knew it was too late. The school was supposed to hold an important student meeting in the step room this afternoon, but I was delayed because of something. When I arrived, the meeting had already begun. The most embarrassing thing was that my arrival caused a brief silence.

At that time, the class was divided again and the students were not familiar with each other. I hurried to find a seat for my class. But that's what made me know her. Then the meeting continued. But as the meeting went on, my tiredness was aroused. In order to dispel my tiredness, I talked with the people nearby. She, like me, was bored with the meeting. We talked to the end of the meeting without disturbing it. As for what we talked about, it was just a daily chat.

The year after that, she and I were just greeting each other daily. It ma de me think that perhaps every ordinary day we live d may be a continuous miracle.

A year later, she and I formed a team for a project. Through this project, I have learned a lot of things that cannot be known only through daily greetings. She was warm and lively, unlike the quiet and reserved way she greeted me. She doesn't laugh at everyone. And most importantly, I think she's the angel in my eyes -- every time I see her, my heart fills with joy.

I decided to tell her when the project was over.

On that day, it was the time for the cherry blossoms to fall and the sky was covered with drops of rain. On the long slope, I said, "well, I hear cherry blossoms are falling at a speed of five centimeters per second . "

She said, " well . So what?"

I said, " you say with this speed, when can I enter your heart?"

When she heard this, she knew that I was expressing my love to her , and then face d a red, bowed head and d id not dare to look me in the eye . After a brief silence, she raised her head slowly, her face slightly flushed, and said, " can you give me a night to think about it?" I said yes.

That night, my heart was pounding and I didn't know when I would fall asleep.

On the next day, I thought that she would give me her answer in the classroom immediately, but she took a sick leave. I thought she would not come today, let alone give me an answer. To my great surprise, she was waiting for me on the long slope .

My heart was filled with joy at seeing her. I know she came to answer me. It was as if she knew I was going to cross the slope .

She saw me, too, and walked slowly up to me, saying in her usual tone, “ I think. . . You're a good guy, but let's stay friends." I was not listening to the rest of what she said. I left the slope at a heavy pace, and she did not catch up.

This feeling is like a dream, a crystal clear dream. And now, I wake up.

After that, it should be no later.
